The quarterly evaluation process continues operating with the majestic unpredictability of a weather system controlled entirely by motivational speakers. Employees who consistently solve difficult problems receive polite acknowledgement, while those responsible for creating the problems are enthusiastically praised for visible participation during meetings and presentations. This unique interpretation of merit has established an environment where competence is treated almost as a private hobby rather than a professional advantage deserving recognition. Naturally, morale fluctuates somewhere between quiet resignation and carefully concealed laughter during executive announcements regarding productivity improvements. Even so, leadership confidently describes the system as fair, transparent.
