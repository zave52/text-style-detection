Corporate governance requires a clear division of responsibilities among board members and executives. A board is tasked with establishing the strategic vision and ensuring fiduciary compliance for shareholders. Conversely, the executive team implements these directives through daily operational management. To maintain independent oversight, the roles of the CEO and Chairperson should be occupied by different individuals. Regular committee meetings and transparent financial reporting are fundamental practices that prevent conflicts of interest. Ultimately, robust governance frameworks protect stakeholder investments and promote ethical enterprise growth.
