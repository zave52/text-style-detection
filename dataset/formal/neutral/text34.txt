Preserving historical documents necessitates strict environmental controls in museums' archives. Fluctuations in humidity and temperature accelerate the decay of parchment and paper materials. Specialized climate systems are thus installed to maintain optimal atmospheric conditions. Archivists utilize acid-free enclosures to prevent chemical degradation over extended periods. Exposure to ultraviolet light is minimized through rigid filtered lighting. Curators wear cotton gloves during physical handling to block the transfer of harmful skin oils. These meticulous conservation strategies ensure that invaluable cultural artifacts remain intact for future academic study.
