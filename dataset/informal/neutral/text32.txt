Recently, I started taking short breaks during work instead of sitting in front of the screen for hours without moving. Usually, I just walk around the apartment, make tea, or open the window for a few minutes to get fresh air. At first, it felt unproductive because I thought breaks would only waste time, but the opposite happened. I noticed that focusing became easier, especially during longer tasks that normally felt exhausting after a while. Small habits like this seem unimportant at first, yet they slowly improve daily routines without requiring huge changes or complicated plans that are difficult to maintain consistently over time.
