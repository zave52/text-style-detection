Yesterday, I decided to take a different route while walking home instead of using the same streets I normally pass every day. The change itself was small, but it made the whole walk feel less repetitive and more interesting. I noticed several stores and cafes that I had never paid attention to before, even though they were not far away at all. Sometimes routines become so automatic that people stop observing what is around them. By the time I arrived home, the walk felt longer in a good way, almost like a short break from regular daily habits. It made me realize that even minor changes in routine can make familiar places feel surprisingly different without requiring anything dramatic or expensive.
