Last weekend, I helped a friend move into a new apartment, and the process turned out to be much more exhausting than expected. We thought it would only take a few hours, but there were far more boxes, bags, and furniture pieces than anyone realized at first. By the middle of the day, everyone was already tired, yet somehow we still kept joking and laughing about the situation. Once everything was finally inside the apartment, we ordered food and sat on the floor because there were barely any chairs unpacked yet. Even though moving is stressful, there was something satisfying about seeing the empty rooms slowly become more personal and comfortable. Experiences like that are tiring in the moment but usually become funny memories later.
