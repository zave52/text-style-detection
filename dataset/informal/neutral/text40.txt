A few nights ago, the electricity suddenly went out in the area for about an hour, which completely changed everyone’s evening plans. At first, people in the building seemed annoyed because there was no internet, no television, and barely any light except for phone flashlights and candles. After a while, though, everything became unexpectedly quiet and calm. I spent some time sitting near the window, listening to sounds from outside and watching how different apartments reacted to the situation. Some neighbors were talking in the hallway, others were laughing, and a few people simply waited patiently until the power returned. It was interesting because moments like that interrupt normal routines and remind people how dependent daily life has become on technology and constant connection.
