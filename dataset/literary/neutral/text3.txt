The cobblestone avenue remained entirely deserted in the early hours of the morning, stretching straight through the commercial district before curving out of sight toward the harbor. The streetlamps, spaced evenly along the paved sidewalk, continued to cast a pale, steady illumination upon the closed storefronts, their mechanical timers not yet triggered to extinguish the lights. A thin layer of mist hovered just above the ground, softening the sharp architectural lines of the brick buildings and slightly dampening the sounds of the distant ocean tide. The display windows of the various shops were completely dark, revealing only vague silhouettes of mannequins and neatly stacked merchandise waiting for the commencement of the business day. An abandoned newspaper lay flat against the damp stone curb, its printed text rendered illegible by the overnight condensation. The ambient temperature was notably crisp, typical for the coastal region during the transition between seasons. There was a profound, undisturbed stillness to the urban environment, a temporary cessation of all human movement and industrial noise as the city simply existed in the quiet, transitional period preceding the impending sunrise.
