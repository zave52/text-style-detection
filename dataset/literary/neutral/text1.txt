The grand library stood in absolute silence, its massive stone columns reaching up toward the vaulted ceiling where faded frescoes depicted ancient maps of the known world. Row upon row of tall, oak bookshelves stretched down the long corridors, perfectly aligned and meticulously organized according to a complex cataloging system established centuries ago. The air was dry and cool, carrying the faint, distinct scent of aged parchment, leather bindings, and settling dust. In the center of the main reading room, a single wooden table sat illuminated by the pale, diffused light filtering through the high clerestory windows. A thick, leather-bound ledger lay open on the desk, its yellowed pages filled with tight, cursive script detailing the daily meteorological observations of a long-forgotten astronomer. The grandfather clock against the far wall ticked with a slow, mechanical precision, measuring the quiet passage of the afternoon in steady, unvarying intervals. Outside, the gray sky remained overcast, offering no indication of the sun's exact position. The entire building existed in a state of suspended preservation, an unchanging repository of historical records waiting passively in the dim, steady light.
