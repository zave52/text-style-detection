The magnificent, peerless knight strode into the grand courtyard with the awe-inspiring grace of a newly born calf trying to navigate a frozen pond. His incredibly expensive, custom-forged plate armor squeaked with every single step, a truly majestic sound that successfully alerted every bandit within a ten-mile radius to his stealthy approach. He paused to strike a heroic pose, gazing thoughtfully into the middle distance, which mostly just gave the local pigeons a perfect stationary target. Naturally, the kingdom's bravest defender had forgotten to actually sharpen his ancestral broadsword, presumably because a dull blade was far more sporting when facing a horde of bloodthirsty barbarians. He mounted his noble steed, a process that miraculously only required three squires, a sturdy stepladder, and twenty minutes of agonizing groans. Once finally seated, he barked a truly inspiring command that sounded suspiciously like a man choking on a grape. It was a genuine comfort to the terrified villagers to know that this absolute marvel of military incompetence was the only thing standing between them and total destruction. We could all sleep soundly knowing our safety rested in the hands of a man who routinely lost arguments with his own horse.
