The ancient and unfathomably wise oracle sat deep within the mystical cavern, surrounded by a thick, suffocating cloud of incense that conveniently obscured the fact that she was simply reading from a poorly written script. Seekers of truth traveled thousands of miles across treacherous mountains just to be blessed with her incredibly profound and cryptic revelations, which usually consisted of brilliant insights like advising them to avoid eating spoiled fish. Her mystical, trance-like state was a truly breathtaking performance, complete with dramatic eye-rolling and mysterious, guttural chanting that sounded suspiciously like someone trying to clear a stubborn throat. For the remarkably affordable price of an entire sack of gold coins, she would graciously peer into her cloudy crystal ball and predict highly specific future events, such as the shocking revelation that it might rain sometime during the spring. The desperate pilgrims would leave the damp cave absolutely weeping with profound gratitude, totally convinced that her vague, nonsensical rambling was the ultimate key to unlocking the deepest secrets of the universe. It was a beautiful, inspiring testament to the boundless limits of human gullibility.
