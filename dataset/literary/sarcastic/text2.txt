Oh, what an absolutely spectacular and unforgettable evening the royal winter gala turned out to be, provided one’s idea of high entertainment involved watching elderly aristocrats chew their flavorless venison for hours on end. The grand ballroom was lavishly decorated in a truly breathtaking shade of depressing gray, a bold artistic choice that perfectly complemented the utterly lifeless atmosphere. The court musicians, demonstrating unparalleled talent, managed to play a remarkably lively waltz at a tempo that closely resembled a funeral dirge. We were generously treated to the scintillating conversation of the Duke of Westbridge, a man who possessed the extraordinary ability to speak continuously for forty-five minutes without conveying a single interesting thought. The crowning achievement of the night, however, was the magnificent feast, which featured a revolutionary culinary masterpiece: soup that was somehow simultaneously boiling hot on the surface and entirely frozen at the bottom. It is a rare and special privilege to spend five hours in a room full of the kingdom's wealthiest nobles, desperately praying for a sudden goblin invasion just to break the agonizing monotony. A truly triumphant celebration of absolute boredom.
