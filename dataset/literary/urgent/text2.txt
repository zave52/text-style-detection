He spurred the exhausted, foaming stallion forward, completely ignoring the dangerous, uneven terrain of the treacherous mountain pass in his desperate race against the fading twilight. The fragile glass vial secured tightly within his leather satchel contained the absolute only known cure for the deadly poison rapidly ravaging the young queen's veins. The royal physician had explicitly stated she would not survive past midnight, and the sun was already bleeding a terrifying, bloody crimson across the western horizon. Icy rain lashed aggressively against his face, nearly blinding him as the horse slipped dangerously on the slick, jagged slate, but he violently yanked the reins and forced the magnificent beast to maintain its breakneck, suicidal pace. Every single wasted second was another agonizing heartbeat closer to the permanent collapse of the entire kingdom. He could already see the distant, flickering watchfires of the capital city desperately signaling through the approaching storm, a frantic beacon demanding his immediate arrival. He leaned low over the horse's neck, praying to any god that would listen for just a little more speed, knowing the fate of thousands rested entirely on his ability to cross those final miles before the last bell tolled.
