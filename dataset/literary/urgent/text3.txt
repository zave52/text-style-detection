The frantic, echoing snap of heavy branches directly behind them confirmed their worst, most terrifying fear: the horrific creatures of the deep wood were rapidly closing the distance. Breathless and bleeding from a dozen jagged briar cuts, she pushed herself wildly through the dense, unforgiving undergrowth, absolutely terrified to even glance over her shoulder at the approaching darkness. The guttural, snarling howls grew exponentially louder with every panicked step, a horrific promise of the brutal, agonizing violence that awaited them if they stumbled even once. They desperately needed to cross the silver river before the blood moon fully crested the jagged treeline, for the protective wards of the sanctuary lay just beyond the rushing water. Her legs burned with a fiery, exhausting pain, threatening to buckle beneath her entirely, but the sheer, blinding adrenaline of raw survival forced her to keep sprinting. She screamed at her wounded companion to never stop moving, practically dragging him forward by the collar of his torn tunic. The terrifying shadows were literally leaping at their heels as they finally broke through the thicket, desperately throwing themselves into the freezing current just as the beasts erupted from the trees.
