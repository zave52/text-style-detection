Our organization recently implemented a sophisticated digital reporting system intended to simplify internal communication. Naturally, employees now spend more time documenting completed tasks than actually completing them. Every minor action requires multiple forms, confirmation emails, and a progress tracker nobody fully understands. Leadership insists the system provides valuable insights, although the only clear result so far is universal confusion and a dramatic increase in spreadsheet usage. Fortunately, executives remain optimistic because the dashboard graphics look extremely professional during shareholder presentations.
