Before modern satellite technology, maritime navigation relied upon a profound understanding of the natural world and precise instruments. Sailors utilized the celestial sphere, measuring the angle of the sun at local noon with a brass sextant to determine their current latitude. At night, the fixed position of the North Star served as an unfailing reference point for vessels traversing the expansive northern oceans. Determining longitude proved a greater challenge, ultimately solved by the invention of the reliable chronometer. This accurate clock allowed navigators to carry the exact time of their home port across vast distances. By calculating the difference between local solar time and the chronometer, a ship could map its coordinates, enabling safer exploration.
