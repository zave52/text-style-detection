The solitary lighthouse stands on a jagged promontory, battered by relentless coastal winds and corrosive salt spray. Its towering white masonry is visibly scarred, bearing the unmistakable marks of numerous winter storms. A rhythmic, piercing beam of light rotates steadily through the accumulating fog, scanning the dark ocean waves. Below, the high tide crashes against the massive basalt rocks, sending white sea foam into the damp air.
