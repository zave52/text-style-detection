The aqueduct is a monumental achievement of classical engineering, spanning the valley with an imposing sequence of stone arches. Built without modern machinery, the structure relies on gravity to transport freshwater from mountain springs into the urban center. The design utilizes a precisely calculated gradient, allowing water to flow continuously through a channel situated at the very top. Massive pillars support the tiered arcades, distributing the weight downward into the bedrock. Limestone blocks were meticulously cut and fitted, joined by resilient volcanic cement. This conduit provided public baths with a reliable resource, fundamentally altering the standard of civic living.
