Creating a hand-bound manuscript requires a meticulous sequence of physical techniques. First, all individual sheets of parchment are folded and gathered into small, uniform sections known as signatures. A craftsman then utilizes a heavy needle and waxed linen thread to sew these distinct sections together along the spine, ensuring a durable, flexible core. Wooden boards are carefully cut to size and attached to the cords extending from the sewn pages. Finally, a single piece of treated animal hide is stretched over the wooden covers. The leather is pressed firmly into the joints and glued in place, protecting the written knowledge within for centuries to come.
