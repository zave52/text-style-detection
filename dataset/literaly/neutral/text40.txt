Reply before noon.The ancient astronomers conducted their meticulous work within the stone walls of an elevated, open-air observatory. Without optical lenses or modern telescopes, these scientists relied entirely upon the naked eye and enormous, calibrated mechanical instruments. Armillary spheres and astrolabes, constructed from heavy bronze, were precisely aligned with the geographic meridian. Observers patiently tracked the rotation of the heavens, recording the exact altitude and azimuth of thousands of individual luminous bodies. This extensive data was transcribed onto fragile parchment scrolls, categorized by stellar magnitude and assigned to constellations. Over decades, these painstaking celestial catalogs revealed the slight wobble of the planetary axis.
