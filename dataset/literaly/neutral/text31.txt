The grand wooden clock stands within the shadowed hallway, its heavy brass pendulum swinging in a constant, unyielding rhythm. Carved from dense mahogany, the tall casing bears the subtle wear of previous generations. Behind the fragile glass pane, polished weights descend fraction by fraction, governed entirely by gravity and the precise arrangement of internal gears. The pale face of the clock exhibits Roman numerals, painted in dark ink upon a faded enamel background. Two slender hands traverse this circular track, calculating the passage of hours without pause. The mechanical heart ticks with an even tempo.
