Sedimentary rock forms through the accumulation of mineral particles and organic materials over vast stretches of geological time. The process begins when existing stones are subjected to prolonged weathering, breaking down into fine sand, silt, and clay. These particles are subsequently transported by water, wind, or glacial ice, settling into distinct layers within deep basins. As millennia pass, the sheer weight of subsequent deposits exerts immense pressure, compacting the lowest structural sediments. Dissolved minerals in groundwater act as a natural cement, binding the loose, scattered grains together into hardened stone.
