Your magnificent poem concerning the existential dread of a common garden snail is a triumph of literature. I am truly staggered by the way you dedicated eighteen excruciating stanzas to describing the slow, damp trail of mucus left upon a single cabbage leaf. One must marvel at the colossal vanity required to assume that mankind has desperately awaited such an unbearably tedious revelation. Truly, it is a masterclass in the fine art of thoroughly destroying the reader's patience.
