I must extend my sincerest sympathies to the brilliant horologist who constructed this incredibly magnificent timepiece. Your decision to replace the traditional hour hand with a mechanical golden pheasant that loudly shrieks a Latin proverb every seventeen minutes is a stunning triumph of utterly useless engineering. It is a rare privilege to own a clock that requires a dedicated staff of three highly trained monks merely to keep its hundreds of spinning gears from spontaneously combusting. One can only hope that knowing the exact lunar phase of Jupiter provides enough profound comfort to offset the maddening, ceaseless symphony of clinking bronze.
