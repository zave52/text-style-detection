Your recent theatrical production regarding the tragic life of a deeply misunderstood turnip has surely left a profound mark on the epic history of dramatic arts. Never before have I witnessed an audience weep so openly, though I strongly suspect those tears were provoked entirely by the thick cloud of dust erupting from the decaying stage curtains. The fact that the lead actor forgot every single line only enhanced the agonizing authenticity of a vegetable silently awaiting its inevitable demise in the hot stewing pot.
