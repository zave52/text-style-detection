The faculty senate wishes to formally congratulate the university administration on their truly visionary decision to drastically reduce the annual research funding for the entire humanities department. It is an absolute stroke of administrative genius to boldly assume that groundbreaking sociological research can be seamlessly conducted using nothing but sheer willpower and discarded library books. We are particularly amazed by the accompanying memo, which cheerfully suggested that we simply leverage our existing intellectual capital, a magnificent piece of corporate jargon that elegantly masks the complete evaporation of our operational budget. Clearly, the sudden removal of our graduate teaching assistants will grant us the wonderful, highly sought-after opportunity to personally grade five hundred undergraduate essays every single week, thereby keeping us closely connected to the pure essence of pedagogy. We eagerly anticipate the upcoming mandatory seminar on maximizing research output with zero financial resources, as we are all absolutely desperate to learn how to magically conjure peer-reviewed publications out of thin air to satisfy the administration’s insatiable metric demands.
