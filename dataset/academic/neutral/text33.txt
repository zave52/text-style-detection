The modern analysis of urban mass transit systems highlights the fundamental correlation between infrastructure investment and regional socioeconomic mobility. To accurately assess this complex relationship, civil engineers collected comprehensive ridership datasets from five major metropolitan districts over a continuous twelve-month period. These longitudinal records were subjected to rigorous multivariate regression modeling to exactly isolate the variables influencing commuter behavior. This academic paper details the mathematical framework utilized during the data processing phase, providing the objective methodology for future civic planning initiatives.
