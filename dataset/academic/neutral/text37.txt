The integration of autonomous navigation systems into civilian maritime logistics requires an exhaustive evaluation of existing international maritime laws. Legal scholars have systematically analyzed a repository of historical maritime jurisprudence to identify potential liability conflicts arising from uncrewed cargo vessel operations. The preliminary findings indicate a significant regulatory gap regarding the precise legal definition of a ship's master within a fully automated digital context. This peer-reviewed article proposes a structured series of legislative amendments designed to modernize these archaic maritime regulations, ensuring the safe and legally compliant deployment of autonomous shipping fleets in the next decade.
