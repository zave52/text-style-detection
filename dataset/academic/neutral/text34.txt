The rigorous evaluation of soil nutrient depletion within high-yield agricultural zones is a critical component of ensuring long-term global food security. Agronomists systematically extracted core soil samples from sixty randomized testing grids across the central farming region during the harvesting season. Laboratory analyses precisely measured the residual concentrations of vital chemical elements, including nitrogen and potassium, to establish a reliable baseline. This extensive empirical study proposes a series of calibrated crop rotation schedules designed to naturally restore localized soil fertility without relying heavily upon synthetic commercial fertilizers.
