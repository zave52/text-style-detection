The implementation of aerodynamic regulations within the commercial aviation industry has significantly altered modern aircraft design architectures. Aerospace engineers are now required to utilize computational fluid dynamics software to simulate wind resistance across various fuselage profiles before physical prototyping begins. These digital simulations generate massive quantities of valuable aerodynamic data, which must be analyzed to successfully identify optimal lift-to-drag ratios. This detailed technical report reviews the underlying mathematical algorithms powering these simulation programs, providing a rigorous structural framework for evaluating the computational accuracy of aerodynamic modeling techniques.
